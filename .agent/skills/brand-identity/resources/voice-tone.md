# ClearFlow: Voice & Tone Guidelines (Hebrew)

## Brand Personality

ClearFlow is a premium B2B automation agency targeting Israeli SMBs. The brand voice is:

- **Authoritative** — We know what we're talking about. No fluff.
- **Direct** — Say it plainly. Israelis don't like filler.
- **Empathetic** — We understand operational pain. Speak at eye level.
- **Premium but not distant** — Smart and professional, never corporate-cold.

## Language Rules (Hebrew)

- **Primary language:** Hebrew (עברית). All user-facing copy must be in Hebrew unless specifically English UI elements (e.g., CTA buttons may use simple Hebrew words).
- **Tone:** Conversational but professional. Use "אתם/אנחנו" (plural you/we), not formal "כם" forms.
- **Avoid slang** — No memes, no emojis in copy. Occasional "תכל'ס" or similar sabra phrases are acceptable for CTAs only.
- **Sentence length:** Short. Max 2 lines per sentence. Break long thoughts into bullets.

## Grammar & Mechanics

| Rule | Standard |
|---|---|
| **Headings (H1, H2)** | Title case equivalent in Hebrew — first word capitalized |
| **Subheadings (H3+)** | Sentence case |
| **Exclamation points** | Avoid in body copy. Acceptable sparingly in CTAs |
| **Voice** | Active, not passive |
| **Numbers** | Use digits (5, not חמש) for scan-ability |

## Page Copy Structure (PAS Model)

When writing section copy, follow this order:
1. **Pain** — Name the operational problem at eye level (2–3 lines)
2. **Agitation** — Show the cost of inaction briefly
3. **Solution** — Present ClearFlow's answer clearly, in bullets when possible
